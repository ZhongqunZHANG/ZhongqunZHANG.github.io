About Myself


I'm a first-year Ph.D. student at the Department of Computer Science, University of Birmingham, supervised by Dr. Hyung Jin Chang and Prof. Ales Leonardis. Prior to the Birmingham, I did my master study (2017-2020) at Tongji University, supervised by Prof. Qirong Tang, and my banchelar (2013-2017) at Harbin Engineering University, China.

My research is about Human-Centred Visual Computing. Driven by solving real-world problems, I study machine (deep) learning to process visual data as developing techniques for sensing, understanding, and serving the human user. Currently, I am working on 3D hand-object pose estimation and corresponding applications in AR/VR or human-computer interaction. Also, I am broadly interested in human pose estimation, eye gaze estimation, visual SLAM and robotics.
